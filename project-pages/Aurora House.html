[Aurora House]

The SOLAR DECATHLON CHINA (SDC) Competition is a renowned global competition aimed at inspiring designers to develop sustainable and innovative residential projects. This competition takes place in various cities across China, providing a platform for participants to showcase their creativity and expertise. In the 2021-2022 edition, the third SDC was hosted in Zhangjiakou city. The competition is open to students from both China mainland and other countries, fostering an international exchange of ideas and encouraging cross-cultural collaboration in the pursuit of sustainable design solutions.

Our team and the team from DTU (Denmark Technical University) collaborated to form a joint team. The DTU team successfully accomplished the initial conceptual design scheme, while our team took charge of the design deepening and construction phases. This collaborative effort allowed us to combine our expertise and resources, resulting in a comprehensive and well-executed project.

After on-site construction, the official competition has been postponed to August 2022 due to the worsening of the pandemic. Thanks to the thorough preparations made beforehand, we successfully completed the rapid construction process within 20 days during the summer of 2021.

Rapid On-site Construction
Design Development

Aurora House comprises an 80-square-meter family residence and a 60-square-meter family residence, connected by a 35-square-meter courtyard. The square floor plan allows for efficient utilization of the space, while minimizing energy and transportation costs.

Prefabricated Modules - Enabling Rapid On-site Construction

Box-type light steel structures achieve a high level of prefabrication for primary unit modules, including the pre-installation of wall insulation and pipelines in the factory. This enables rapid on-site construction. To optimize transportation efficiency and minimize costs, the primary units are divided into modules with dimensions matching the maximum width of large cargo trucks (typically 3.6m). As a result, only four trucks are needed to transport the entire building structure.

Parametric Facade Design & AR Assisted Construction - Parametric Facade Design

The parametric facade skin controls the tilting angle of the steel bamboo tiles through curve interference, creating a delightful and personalized visual effect.

Implementing a parametric facade presents challenges in manufacturing, material selection, and potential performance impacts. Customized components and intricate techniques are required for precise assembly. Material selection must consider feasibility, cost, and durability. Moreover, parametric facades can affect energy efficiency and overall building performance. After iterative testing and careful consideration, we made the decision to use steel bamboo material with a simple texture. This choice struck a balance between ease of construction, cost-effectiveness, and performance considerations.

Augmented Reality (AR) Assisted Construction

To construct parametric facades, frequent drawing consultation were necessary. We employed augmented reality (AR) technology to overlay models onto walls in the real world, facilitating visualization. By differentiating tiles with colors, we eliminated the need for measuring, marking, and repeatedly reviewing drawings, significantly improving efficiency and accuracy.

During Bamboo roof assembly, we faced substantial discrep- ancies between construction and design, a common chal- lenge in real-world projects with challenging quantification of cumulative errors. With AR technology, we effectively ad- dressed the errors and imple- mented a remedial plan under my leadership, leveraging the visualization of the errors.
