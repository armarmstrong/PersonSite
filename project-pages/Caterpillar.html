The History of Snake Game

The snake game is a classic game genre that has been around for over 40 years. It is a simple game that requires the player to move a snake around the screen, eat food, and avoid hitting the walls, itself, or other snakes. The snake game is a simple game that is easy to learn and enjoy.

I would call it a "meta game" because it was born very early, just 4 years after "Pong", the first video game in the world. It was originally called "Blockade", an arcade game built by Gremlin engineer Lane Hauck in 1976.

"Blockade" was a hit as soon as it was released, however, after a series of business crises and plagiarism, the innovator Lane Hauck was forced to design a new game "Hustle" based on it, which was more similar to the classic snake game. Then, a bundle of games designed based on "Blockade" and "Hustle" were released.

It wasn't until 1980 that someone finally noticed that the longer and longer object in these games looked like a snake, and they were called "snake" games.

In 1995, Nokia designer Taneli Armanto was coincidentally assigned to develop a game for the new Nokia 6110. He successfully transplanted the snake game to the new platform. This game became the world's first truly popular mobile game. It even supported multiplayer battles. Hundreds of millions of people played this game on Nokia phones.

From then on, the snake game has been one of the world's most popular games. Many derivative games have been created, including "Snake 2", "Snake Xenzia", "Snake 3D", and "Slither.io".
