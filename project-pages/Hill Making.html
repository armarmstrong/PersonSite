- An Emotional Journey in Piled Stone Hills: Space, Experience, and Interactive Design

[Hill Making]

“假山” (piled stone hill) also known as artificial mountains, are an integral element in traditional Chinese gardens, occupying a unique space between the artificial and the natural. They are believed to have originated from the imaginative realm of the place of gods. As the literati class began constructing private gardens, they materialized the essence of nature distilled in their refined landscape paintings.

Problems to Solve

Problem [1]

Piled stone hills hold a significant place in Chinese culture, yet they remain shrouded in mystery, representing an esoteric discipline. The intricacies of their construction and design often prove challenging for the general public to comprehend. Unfortunately, the scarcity of data and research regarding this subject has hindered the advancement of this field.

Problem [2]

Additionally, piled stone hills serve as traditional entertainment spaces and are widely sought after by tourists and visitors. However, in today's digital age, the pure spatial encounter they offer may not be as novel as it once was.

[CHAPTER 1] Hill Hacking

Typological Study - Space in Piled Stone Hills

The first step is to understand the typological features of piled stone hills. I have studied the history of piled stone hills, their cultural significance, and their architectural features.

Utilizing Grasshopper, I developed a plugin that effortlessly generates 3D models showcasing various spatial arrangements within piled stone hills, solely relying on nodes and directions.

Connecting the Dots - Space Syntax for Hills

The next challenge is to combine these spaces. Inspired from Spatial Syntax and topological graph theory, I have devised a new graph for this issue, this graph is:
• planer - for readability;
• undirected - no clear direction in hills;
• cyclic - occasionally cyclic;
• weighted - representing length;
I have employed this graph to analyze several famed piled stone hills, confirming its feasibility in research and design.

Combination

The spatial information of lesser-known piled stone hills remains inadequately explored， creating a research gap in this field.
With more available data, it becomes possible to integrate Machine Learning to enhance the spatial design of piled stone hills in the future.

[PART 2] Digital Hill

Solution - Creating a Novel Experience

Inspired from Slow House (DS+R - 1989) and House of Dust (Alison Knowles & James Tenneyt - 1967), I attempted to explore how media enhances human experiences in space. By creating a feedback system, a loop is formed among nature, senses and emotions.

System Design - Hardware and Data Flow

The system design is based on the feedback loop, which is composed of three components:
• Data Collection: Collecting data from sensors, including audio, video, and biometric data;
• Backend Processing: Processing the data and generating projection materials;
• Frontend Presentation: Using projector, LED matrix and audio system to create a visual and auditory experience.

I have designed a prototype of the Sensus Stone - Module Unit based on GSR & PPG Sensors. The system is designed to capture the user’s emotional state according to the GSR and PPG signals, balencing the expense and accuracy of the system.

Emotion Classification

The approach confirms the viability of real-time emotion classification using lightweight models in limited hardware. Despite data and detail limitations preventing full replication, it serves as a valuable and essential reference supporting my project.
